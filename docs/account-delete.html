Delete Your Account

You can permanently delete your Family Rights App account and all associated data at any time. This page explains how, and what happens to your data when you do.

Warning: this cannot be undone. Deleting your account permanently removes all your cases, documents, evidence, timeline entries, and profile information. There is no recovery option after deletion is confirmed.

Option 1: Delete in the app (recommended)

This is the fastest way. The deletion is immediate and automatic.

1. Open the Family Rights App on your device.
2. Tap the Settings tab (bottom navigation bar, rightmost tab).
3. Scroll to Delete Account and tap the button.
4. Read the warning and tap Continue.
5. Enter your password to confirm your identity.
6. Tap Permanently Delete My Account.

Your account, all cases, documents, and evidence are deleted within seconds.

Option 2: Email request

If you no longer have access to the app or your account, send a deletion request from the email address associated with your account to: privacy@poordudeholdings.com

Include the subject line: "Account Deletion Request". We will verify your identity and complete the deletion within 30 days. You will receive a confirmation email when your account has been deleted.

What gets deleted

  • Your account profile (name, email, region)
  • All cases and case metadata
  • All parties, events, and timeline entries
  • All uploaded documents and evidence files
  • All issue flags and pattern matches
  • All exports and attorney review requests
  • Your subscription entitlement record and purchase history
  • Any coalition opt-in records and content reports

If you published any reference cases to the public library, those cases will be force-unpublished and removed at the same time. Your account is then permanently deleted from Appwrite and Google Play. Your subscription (if active) must be separately canceled in Google Play settings — cancellation is not automatic.

Data we cannot delete

Anonymized, aggregated pattern statistics that do not contain identifying information may be retained per our Privacy Policy. Audit logs may be retained for up to 7 years as required by applicable law. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
